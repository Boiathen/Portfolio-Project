Why Networking?

I am a rising senior at North High School. Prior to my Junior year, I was selected for the Cisco Networking Program at the Southern Indiana Career and Technical Center (SICTC). While at SICTC my teacher, Mr. Devault, ignited my passion for networking. In this class, I did a lot of things, but one that stood out was when I completed a lab on Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol (DHCP). Within the DHCP lab I created and programmed a network of switches, routers, and computers perfectly. The success I achieved showed me that networking was fun and I was good at it. That experience solidified my future goals to attend Murray State University, major in Cybersecurity Management, and obtain my CCNA. Doing so would allow me to achieve my dream job of being a Cisco network engineer (or a network engineer).
